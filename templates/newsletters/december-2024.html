1. Hyderabad Conference

The Let Us Dream Annual Conference 2024

Venue: St. Francis Degree College for Women

Date: 21st September, 2024

Hosted at St. Francis College for Women, the Let Us Dream Annual Conference 2024 brought together thought leaders, innovators, and changemakers to address the theme "Building a Holistic Community: Strategies and Solutions." The conference explored sustainable development through integrated efforts across education, health, and social sectors, with the aim of driving meaningful change at the grassroots level.

Key panels at the conference delved into vital areas of impact:
● The Education Panel focused on the ethical integration of artificial intelligence and the need for curriculum reforms to prepare students for the challenges of the future.
● The Health Panel centered on women’s health, with in-depth discussions on mental health awareness and maternal care.
● The Social Panel highlighted the empowerment of marginalized communities, exploring the importance of resilience and grassroots development in fostering social change.

The Changemakers Session provided powerful insights and inspiration, featuring leaders such as Father Jose, Jaya Bharathi from the MOWO movement, Dr. Sajadullah, who shared his healthcare initiatives, and Subhalakshmi, an advocate for the differently- abled.
By promoting cross-sector collaboration and community engagement, the conference laid a strong foundation for inclusive and sustainable progress, emphasizing the collective action needed to achieve societal transformation.

2. Pune Conference

Inaugural Let Us Dream Annual Community Conference 2024

Venue: Savitribai Phule Pune University

Date:28th February, 2024

The inaugural Let Us Dream Annual Community Conference in Pune, held at Savitribai Phule Pune University, brought together changemakers to address pressing Conference wise content issues in Health, Social Issues, and Education, fostering impactful community development and collaboration.

The Health Panel explored key topics such as digital accessibility, fitness, and diabetes prevention. Notable speakers included Mrs. Tejasvini, CEO of Sugamya Digital Solutions, who inspired attendees with her advocacy for digital literacy and healthcare, despite being visually imparied. Mr. Bhagwat shared valuable insights on physiotherapy, while Dr. Ravindra emphasized the importance of mindful living and healthy eating habits.

The Social Issues Panel addressed critical concerns like human trafficking and inclusivity. Dr. Mathew discussed Maharashtra’s anti-trafficking initiatives, Ms. Suhasini focused on survivor rehabilitation, and Mr. Tushar highlighted the need for equitable education for people with disabilities.

The Education Panel underscored the significance of inclusive learning. Dr. Pharande reflected on his work in Jammu and Kashmir, Dr. Sadakale advocated for rural education, and Ms. Patwardhan emphasized the importance of vocational training.

Through these dynamic discussions, the conference provided a platform for collaboration and actionable solutions, advancing the cause of community-driven development.

3. Bangalore - Conference

Youth and Employability Conference 2024

Venue: St. Francis De Sales (Autonomous) College

Date: 19th October, 2024

The Youth and Employability Conference , hosted by St. Francis De Sales College (Autonomous) in collaboration with Jyoti Nivas College and Christ (Deemed to be University), brought together a diverse group of experts to tackle the critical challenge of workforce readiness.


Esteemed speakers, including Ms. Suja Warriar, Director of Academia at Wequity, Ms. Suma Merlin, Assistant Professor at St. Francis De Sales College, and Mr. Venu Doddavarapu, Founder of Theatre for Liberation, emphasized the importance of innovative educational strategies. Their discussions covered a range of vital topics, from fostering sustainability and diversity to integrating experiential and social-emotional learning frameworks, such as the Delhi Government’s Happiness Curriculum.


The conference highlighted key themes such as skill development, industry-academia collaboration, and entrepreneurial innovation. By focusing on these areas, the event aimed to bridge the gap between education and employability, ensuring that young individuals are well-equipped to meet the evolving demands of the modern workforce.


The host institutions, renowned for their commitment to excellence, provided a platform for actionable solutions and transformative ideas, empowering youth for a brighter future.

4.Chennai Conference

Let Us Dream Annual Community Conference 2024
Fostering Collaboration for a Sustainable Future

The Let Us Dream (LUD) Annual Community Conference 2024 hosted by Women’s Christian College brought together over 608 participants, including students, researchers, volunteers, NGO leaders, and thought experts, for meaningful discussions and collaborations.

Themed “A Holistic Approach to Sustainable Well-Being”, the conference featured interactive panel discussions, engaging dialogues, and impactful workshops. Participants, including students from 13 colleges, explored real-world social challenges, collaborative projects, and sustainability initiatives.

The conference showcased the contributions of 80+ NGOs and inspired participants with stories of resilience and innovation. The event emphasized fostering compassion, practical experience, and holistic growth, empowering individuals to become future- ready leaders.

>Key speakers like Arun Fernandez, founder of Blink Foundation, and Ponni Bharatanatyam, a distinguished dancer and trainer, underscored the importance of community-driven action and sustainability. WCC Principal Lilian I. Jasper highlighted the conference's role in shaping students into socially responsible leaders, prepared to address community challenges.

5. Louisiana Conference

Inaugural Let Us Dream Conference at Grambling State University

Venue: Grambling State University

Date: 31st October, 2024

On October 31, 2024, Grambling State University hosted the inaugural Let Us Dream Conference at the Fredrick C. Hobdy Assembly Center, bringing together students, educators, and community leaders to promote social justice, cultural competence, and community empowerment.


The conference featured powerful panel discussions, cultural performances, and poignant moments, including a heartfelt tribute to the late Tori Smith, chair of the LUD 2024 social committee. Madison Mason’s poem, What a Beautiful Gift It Is to Let Us Dream, inspired attendees with a message of resilience and hope.


Fr. Lijo Thomas, founder of Let Us Dream Inc., emphasized the importance of collaboration in weaving community stories into actionable models for change. Grambling President Martin Lemelle Jr. and other university leaders highlighted the significance of cross-cultural exchanges and global awareness. Panel discussions focused on initiatives to foster inclusion, equity, and safety in education, while Deputy Sheriff Gary Guice introduced the Dream Program, enabling students who are mostly overlooked to discover their voices and achieve their goals to the fullest.


The conference underscored the transformative power of dreaming together, creating pathways for inclusive and empowered communities.

6. New York Conference

Inaugural Let Us Dream Conference at Grambling State University

Venue: Binghamton University

Date: 8th November, 2024

The Let Us Dream Annual Community Conference 2024 at Christ Nagar College The Let Us Dream Annual Community Conference 2024 at Christ Nagar College, Maranalloor opened with powerful addresses from key dignitaries. Ms. Deepthi Rani SS welcomed attendees, recognizing figures such as Raju Narayana Swamy IAS and Fr. Lijo Thomas CMI.

Fr. Health panelists Dr. Ajan M. J. and Dr. Athira Menon spoke on community health and holistic approaches to reproductive health. The conference inspired collaboration for meaningful community change.
